Louis Falisse is a Communication Designer from Brussels, Belgium. Louis practices graphic, editorial, and web design with a passion in photography, architecture, and product design. Inspired by European art movements such as brutalism and bauhaus, Louis’ work is intentional and functional with an emphasis on proportion and alignment.

Our (Communal) Frame of Sight

Editorial Publication (Typology)

In Progress

Hey there ladies and gents, as many of you may know I’ve been working on a ‘window’ series for my thesis this year.

As of late i’ve been wanting to capture every window, an impossible task. Instead I created this two step book, ‘Our (Communal) Frame of Sight’, with the intention of producing a workbook providing readers with context on my works as well as a request for your individual participation.

I would love for you all to participate by capturing window images, and forwarding them to me. I will then edit, classify, and archive all the images I receive into our very own community archive book,‘Our (Communal) Frame of Sight’.

Book Transcript


My dear reader, It is with sincere hope of understanding that this publication was (and will be) collected, categorized, and compiled. A hope that one may achieve a heightened awareness to the power of framing and the occasional lack thereof. Windows are not to be reduced to a simple passage of light, they are to be praised for everything more that they hold.


With this project I’d like to stress the beauty of human thought. The slight uniqueness and vivid purpose in each brick laid. The individual thought and process to each frame, each glass pane, each fragment of view.

With this booklet, pamphlet, map; I’d like to take advantage of this unique opportunity afforded to us by covid to arrange a community made archive, collection, book.


Some tips are below to help your image taking process.
To capture straight images, start by heading to your phone settings and locating the camera settings, continue by toggling the “grid” on.

After stumbling upon a window of interest, hold your arms out above and in front of you and attempt to capture a focused, and relatively flat image.

Look for: light, shadow, gates, bricks, interior, exterior.

there’s no need to go on a trip, Many of my photographs are from my own neighborhood and i’d love to see how you capture yours.
